Message Tips and Ideas

The success of any new communication endeavor depends on how it is received by students, parents, staff and the community. This section is designed to share the experience of our staff in developing comprehensive communication strategies. The following tips and ideas will help you get the most from the system.

Tips for sending effective messages:

  • Write your message down before recording it: To create a professional sounding recording that flows smoothly, always script your message first. Also, have someone else read the script and give feedback before sending the message.

  • Make your first call a positive one: Rather than making your first call an absence notification, start by calling all parents with a short message introducing them to the messaging system. Briefly explain how it will be used to provide them with relevant and timely information.

  • Be professional, but do not be afraid to have fun: Many schools have found that they can achieve the goal of getting important information delivered while putting a smile on a parent’s face.

  • Always repeat important information such as dates and times of events several times throughout your message: Doing so allows recipients time to write down important information.

  • Start your messages by identifying who you are and the reason for the message: To ensure that recipients immediately recognize the message as coming from your school or district, start each message by identifying yourself and the nature of the call.

  • Practice your recordings until you find the most effective speaking volume: As with any type of recording, the volume of your voice and the distance at which you position the microphone affect the quality of the sound. By practicing your recordings before sending messages to parents, you can find the combination that works best for you.

  • Do not be afraid of feedback from your recipients: Ask recipients if the messages you are sending are clear and how they could be improved. Also find out the type of information recipients desire and how often they want to hear from you.

Application Ideas

In addition to delivering effective attendance messages, you can use the system for many types of communication between school and home. The following is a list of just a few of the ways that you can put it to work for you. Start by reviewing your calendar of upcoming events. Note every significant event and time call outs around these dates. Be prepared to send messages for any of the following:

Academic

  • Progress report call outs

  • Homework & test reminders

  • Report-card mailing announcements

  • Honor Roll announcements

  • Registration notices

  • College Night reminders

Disciplinary and Administrative

  • Attendance notifications

  • Tardy notifications

  • Wake-up calls ("Tardy Club")

  • Detention reminders

  • Meeting reminders

  • Overdue obligation reminders

 

Community Involvement

  • Congratulatory messages

  • Immunization reminders

  • Open House / Back to School invitations

  • PTO & PTA reminders & invites

  • Athletic, band, drama and other group announcements

  • Picture Day reminders

  • Field trip reminders

  • Bond/Levy Vote reminders

Safety and Logistics

  • Snow day announcements

  • Emergency broadcasts

  • School closures

Sample Messages

The following samples are intended to give you some ideas for messages you could use.

  • Attendance Message: This is Mr. Price, the principal of Anytown Elementary School, calling to let you know that your child was marked absent today. Please remember to call or send a signed note explaining the reason for this absence. If you have any questions please feel free to contact Mrs. Guy, the school's attendance secretary, at (123) 456-7890. Thank you.

  • Attendance Message With Text-to-Speech: This is Mrs. Smith, the principal of Anytown High School, calling to let you know that your child <Insert Student Name> was marked absent from school today for the following periods, <Insert Periods Missed>. Please remember to send a signed note upon their return to school explaining the reason for this absence. If you have any questions regarding this message please feel free to contact Mr. Guy, the school's attendance secretary at (123) 456-7890. Thank you.

  • Special Event Announcement: Hello, this is Mr. Thomas, the principal of Franklin Middle School, calling to remind you of our upcoming open house taking place this Tuesday, March 15th beginning at 6:00 PM in the school gym. This year's event promises to be both informative and entertaining as you get an opportunity to meet your child's teachers and view many of the projects and accomplishments that have been achieved by our student body. There will also be performances by our award winning jazz band and our drama club beginning at 7:00 PM. We truly hope that you will make every effort to attend and show your support for our school and your child's education. If you would like more information, please contact the school office at 123-4567. Once again, this is Mr. Thomas reminding you of our open house on Tuesday, March 15th at 6:00 PM in the gym. We look forward to seeing you there.

  • Holiday Reminders: Hello, this Christmas greeting is from the office staff at Wilson Elementary who would like to wish you and your family a wonderful holiday season. We truly appreciate the opportunity to work with you and your child and look forward to an exciting and productive second half of the year. If there's anything we can do to help your child be more successful, please feel free to contact us. Also, don't forget that school resumes on Monday, January 4th. We hope you have a happy and restful holiday.

  • Birthday Messages: Hello, this is Mrs. Henry, the principal at 4th Street Elementary, calling to wish <Insert Student's Name> a happy birthday. I consider it an honor to have you as a member of our student body and would like to express my appreciation of your efforts at our school by giving you a gift. Please see either me or the school secretary tomorrow and mention that you received this greeting message. We've got a birthday front-of-the-line lunch pass just for you in honor of this special occasion. Thanks again for all you do. I look forward to seeing you tomorrow.

  • Standardized Testing Reminders: Hello, this is Mr. McDonald, the principal of George Washington Middle School calling to remind all of our parents and students that we begin our SAT9 testing on Tuesday, April 25th. The SAT9 is a standardized test taken by all students in the state covering the areas of math, language, and reading. It will be given on four consecutive days, taking approximately two hours each morning from Tuesday through Friday. Due to the importance of the test, we hope that you will make every effort to have your child at school, well rested, and focused. If for some reason they cannot attend on any of the days, makeup exams will be given the following week. We hope that you will support your child by explaining the significance of the testing and by encouraging them to put forth their best effort. If you have any questions please contact the school office at 123-4567. Once again, the SAT9 testing begins this Tuesday, April 25th. Thank you for your continuing support of our school program.

  • Emergency Notifications: Hello, this is Mr. Williams calling all of the staff of John Hart High School with an important announcement. I sincerely regret to inform you that school will be canceled today as a result of the continuing snow fall. While I know this comes as a tremendous disappointment to you all, please try to contain your emotions so as not to wake up your neighbors. You can trust me when I say that I'm as distraught about this news as you are. But never fear! We have every intention of holding classes tomorrow unless you hear differently from me.

  • Course Test Reminders: Hi, this is Mrs. Ruiz at San Rafael High calling to remind all of my Freshman English students and their parents about this Friday's midterm exam. This test covers chapters seven through twelve as well as the current events handouts. Parents, you can help by reviewing the study guide questions with your child. If you ask to see the guide and they say, ”r;What study guide?”, tell them it's the two-sided one, on blue paper, that says Freshman English Mid-Term Study Guide across the top that was handed out on Monday with five minutes left in the class period. That should jog their memory! Let me end by saying thank you for all the help. I truly enjoy working with all of you, and I look forward to a successful midterm exam and a productive remainder of the year. If you have any questions please feel free to call me at (123) 456-7890, and I'll get back to you as soon as I can. Good Bye.

  • Volunteer Requests: Hello, this is Mary Sanchez, the president of the Bobcat Boosters for Lincoln Elementary. I'm calling to let you know about the rummage sale fund raiser planned for this Saturday, May 15th starting at 9:00 am in the school parking lot. We could really use volunteers to help organize donations and supervise the sale starting at 8:00 am. If you are able to help for even a few hours please press one, and I will contact you and get you on the schedule. If you can't volunteer we would still love to see you at the sale supporting your school. Thank you for your time. I look forward to seeing you Saturday.
