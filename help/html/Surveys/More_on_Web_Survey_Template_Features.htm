More About Web Survey Features

Important: Only those features that you have been granted access to will display. For access to additional features contact your system administrator.

Any survey can have both a phone and web component. It is important, however, that you use only one Survey Template if you are creating a survey that has both components. The web portion of creating a survey template has only been moved into its own help topic to simplify Survey Template help overall.

Before you begin to assemble the web portion of your survey, you will need to create an Email Invitation message. This message is created like any other Email message. Your message does not need to contain a link to your survey, as one will be generated and appended to the end of the email by the system. You should, however, direct participants to click the link at the bottom of the email to participate.

Sample Message:

Dear Parents,

This is a message from Anytown Elementary inviting you to participate in our automated online survey about school uniforms. It should only take about two minutes. Your opinion matters to us and we would greatly appreciate your participation. Please click the link below to proceed to our survey.

Thank you,

Mrs. Smith
Principal

Adding Web Features to a Survey

Important: Do not use a separate Survey Template for the phone and web versions of your survey.

Once you have created your Email message inviting recipients to participate in your survey, you are ready to add web functionality to your Survey Template. Any survey can have an online component, with or without a phone survey. However, it is important to note that if your survey will feature both a phone and web version, they should be indicated in the same Survey Template.

  1. In your Survey Template, click the Web Survey checkbox as shown in the image above.

  2. Select the Email Message you created to invite recipients to participate in your survey from the Email Message drop down menu.

  3. Enter a title for the survey's web page in the Web Page Title field.

  4. Type a thank you message into the Web thank you message field. This is the message participants will see when they have completed your survey.

  5. Click the checkbox for Use HTML in Web Survey if you would like to use HTML code in your questions or messages. If you do not check this box and use HTML tags like <b></b> to bold text, then the tag will display to your participants.

  6. Enter your questions at the bottom of the page. In the Report Label field, name your question as you would like it to appear in the Survey Report. It's a good idea to use a name indicative of the question's topic to make interpreting the report easier. Note: Make sure your question defines the numeric values of the responses. For example, "From the following list, which color do you prefer? Select 1 for red, 2 for yellow, or 3 for blue."

  7. When you have entered all of the information you may click Save for later, or, if the Survey Template is complete, click Schedule Survey to proceed to the Survey Scheduler.
