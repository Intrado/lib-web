Facebook

Important: This feature is only visible if your system includes the Social Media feature. Social Media is a new feature from SchoolMessenger that allows you to leverage the power of social media to deliver your message. For more information regarding the Social Media feature, please call 1-800-920-3897.

Facebook is a great way to reach out to your contacts who are avid Facebook users. This feature allows you to connect to your Facebook account and post to your Wall or to any Facebook Pages where you are an administrator. Contacts who are your Facebook friends or who have liked your Facebook Page can then see your message when they log in.

Connecting a Facebook Account

You will need to allow the SchoolMessenger Facebook app to have access to your account before you can post. You can do this from the Account link at the top of any page or as you create a job with MessageSender. You will only need to grant permission once. Your permission will last until you deliberately revoke it.

The image below shows the social media options on the Account Settings page, found at the Account link in the upper right corner of any screen.

To authorize a Facebook account, just click the "Connect to Facebook" button. If you're already logged in, the account you're logged into will be authorized for posts. If you're not logged into Facebook, a window will pop up, allowing you to log in and grant permission.

Note: If you are currently logged into a Facebook account, this is the account you will authenticate. If it is not the correct account, please log out before you click "Connect to Facebook". If you've already connected to the account and it is incorrect, please read the section on "Changing Facebook Accounts".

If you haven't connected to Facebook and you're trying to send a job with social media options, you may also access these buttons as a step in MessageSender.

 

Posting to Facebook

Once your Facebook account has been authorized, posting is very simple. Just select where you would like to post and enter a message. The image above shows a message being created for Facebook and Twitter.

Selecting "My Wall" from the options at the top of the page will post your message to the wall on your user account. For example, if you're a principal and you've authorized your own Facebook account, you will post to your personal page. You may also choose to post to any "Facebook Pages" wall where you are an administrator. In the image above, a "Facebook Pages" page is chosen.

When you post to "Facebook Pages", shown below, your post will appear to come from the Page, not from you personally. For example, a Facebook Page for "Springfield School District" would contain posts from "Springfield School District", similar to the Facebook screenshot shown below.

 

Removing a Facebook Account

To remove a Facebook account, go to the Account link in the upper right corner. Click on the "Disconnect from Facebook" button. This disconnects the system from your account. To completely deauthorize our application, you will need to log into your Facebook account and remove the app from your Account settings on Facebook's web site.

Changing Facebook Accounts

If you would like to change which Facebook account you will post to, simply follow the instructions to remove a Facebook account. After the account is removed, you will see the "Connect to Facebook" button again. Make sure you are logged out of the Facebook account, then click the "Connect to Facebook" button. Just log into the correct account and grant permission to post.
