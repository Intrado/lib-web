About Blocked Lists

Important: Only those features that you have been granted access to will display. For access to additional features contact your system administrator.

The Blocked List Feature

The Blocked List feature allows you to block all messages to particular phone numbers and email addresses. It also allows you to identify potentially disconnected numbers and bad email addresses so that you may correct them, improving the accuracy of your data sources. These will appear as phone numbers or email addresses which were automatically blocked by the system.

Where can I find the Blocked List feature and do I need permission to access it?

You will need System Administrator access privileges to access this feature.

To access the blocked lists see the Blocking Phone Numbers  and Blocking Email Addresses  help topics.  

What does "Blocked" in the status column of a report mean?

If you see the status of  "blocked" for a phone number or email address in a job completion report, this means that the number or email address are in the blocked list. It does not mean that the phone company or email provider is blocking the message that you tried to send.

Note: If the report shows "Blocked" for an SMS text message and the number is not on your blocked list, it either means that the recipient has not opted in from their cell phone, giving you permission to send SMS text messages to their phone, or they have explicitly unsubscribed their number from receiving SMS text messages.

How do phone numbers and email addresses get on the blocked list?

Phone numbers and email addresses can only be added to the blocked lists one of two ways.

  1. An individual user manually adds an entry to the list.

  2. The system automatically adds a disconnected phone number or invalid email address to the list.

When should I manually add phone numbers and email addresses to the blocked lists?

You should only add phone numbers and email addresses to the blocked list when it is not possible to resolve the issue by correcting the source data. For example, if a person contacts you to indicate that they are receiving your notification calls, but they do not have any association with the school or district, you should locate the source of the incorrect number (your SIS or HR data systems) and correct the information there.

If you receive a request from a parent who no longer wishes to receive any calls to a particular number or emails to a particular email address, you may choose to add that number to the blocked list with a note indicating the reason.

Which entries in the blocked lists are auto-blocked?  

Auto-blocked phone numbers and email addresses are entries in the blocked lists which are entered by SchoolMessenger. These entries are created when the system confirms that a phone number is disconnected or an email address is invalid. This is determined wither by identifying a pattern of numerous consecutive failed calls over a period of several months or by information sent back from the recipient's email server indicating an invalid email address. The reason for the block is indicated in the description field so it's easy to follow up and correct the source of the bad data.
