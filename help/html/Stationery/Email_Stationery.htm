Creating & Sharing Email Stationery

Important: Only those features that you have been granted access to will display. For access to additional features contact your system administrator.

The email stationery feature lets you get creative and have fun with the appearance of your messages. The system includes several layouts which may be edited and shared with other users who will be able to use it when creating new messages.

This help topic will walk you through the process of creating your own stationery.

Creating Stationery

To get started, you'll need to add new stationery.

Note: If you wish to edit existing stationery, click on the Stationery view on the left side of the screen. Then click the Edit link next to the stationery you wish to edit.

  • Navigate to the Message Builder by clicking on the Broadcasts tab followed by the Messages subtab.

  • Click the Add New Stationery button in the upper right corner.

  • Enter a name and description for your stationery.

  • Select a generic layout to use as a base for your new stationery, as shown below.

  • Click the Next button to start editing.

  • When you've finished editing the stationery click the Done button at the bottom of the editor.

 

Changing Colors

The first thing you may wish to do is to change the colors of the various components.

  • Enter the Theme Manager by clicking on the round theme icon, shown on the right side of the image above. This group of controls can be found in the lower right corner of the editor toolbar.

  • In the Theme Manager window, shown below, select the part of the layout you wish to change and then either select a color from the palette or enter the hex value for the color you want.

  • After you select a color, click OK to make the change in the template.

Idea: If you need a little inspiration, you can create wonderful color schemes with complimentary colors using free color scheme designers online. Some tools can even create palettes from images.

  • Continue doing this until you've changed all of the component colors you'd like to change.

  • In the image below, you can see how the example layout looks once all of the colors have been edited.

 

Replace or Add an Image

Important: It's a best  practice to make sure your images are small enough to fit  within small email clients such as those found on mobile devices. Typically this means ensuring that a design doesn't exceed 600 pixels wide. Also, many email clients do not display images by default so it's a good idea to avoid conveying important parts of your message within an image. There are many excellent resources online for learning more about HTML email design if you wish to learn more.

Some templates, such as the one in this example,  include placeholder images which you'll definitely want to replace. Others may not have an image, but you're still free to add one if you want.

  • To replace an image, double click the image to open the Image options. To add an image, place your cursor where you want the image and click the Image button .

  • If your image is hosted somewhere, just replace the Image URL with the URL for your image. Otherwise, click the Choose File button and select an image from your computer.

  • If you've used an image from your computer, make sure to click the Upload button.

Note: If you choose to upload an image, your file should not exceed 750 kB in size. The best formats to use for images in email are .jpg or .gif.

  • Click OK to finish changing the image.

Freezing Text or Images in Place

Notice that there are dashed lines around some parts of the template. This means that those sections are editable regions. An editable region is a part of the text that the users of your stationery will be able to change.

  • You may "freeze" items in place by removing their editable properties.

  • Click on the editable region you wish to freeze.

  • Next to the theme editor button in the tool bar, you'll find the editable region button, shown in the center of the image below.   

  • Click the editable region button to remove the editable region from the stationery.

The dashed line around the area is now removed. Users will not be able to make changes to this text when they use your template.

 

Sharing Stationery

Once your stationery is complete, you can share it with other users in the system.

  • From Message Builder, click on the Stationery view.

  • Locate the stationery you wish to share and click the Publish link to the right of the name.
