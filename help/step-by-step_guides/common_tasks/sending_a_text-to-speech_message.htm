Sending a Text-to-Speech Message

You can send a message using text-to-speech as an alternative to recording an audio message. This tutorial will show you how to send a basic text-to-speech message.

Note: Before you begin, make sure that you've created a list.

  1. From your Start Page, click the Create New Job button.

  2. Click the Phone icon to open up the Phone options.

  3. Fill in the appropriate information in the Settings section.

  4. Click "Create a text-to-speech message" in the Phone section.

  5. Type your phone message into the text box.

Some tips for making the most of your text-to-speech message:

    • Text-to-Speech works best with simpler sentences.

    • Use correct punctuation and add commas at phrase boundaries. For example: "Due to snow, there will be no school today, but we will be open on Monday." will sound better than, "Due to snow there will be no school today but we will be open on Monday."

    • If you have a complicated sentence, such as the example in the previous tip, and the text-to-speech engine is having difficulty pronouncing it, try breaking it down into simpler sentences. For example: "There will be no school today because of snow. We will return on Monday."

    • Some phrases are well-trained in the text-to-speech engine. For example, try some different greetings with different punctuation such as, "Good morning!" versus, "Good morning."

  1. Click the Play button to preview your message.

  2. If you would like to automatically translate this message so that contacts with other language preferences will receive a call in their preferred language, click "Automatically translate into other languages".

  3. Click the Proceed to Confirmation button when you are done.
