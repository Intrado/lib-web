Reviewing Your Responses

Important: Recipient responses are stored on the system for 30 days before being deleted.

Important: You must instruct recipients to press 0 to leave a message.

When you create a notification or survey job, you may allow your recipients to leave you a message. There are two ways to review the responses for your job.

Using The Actions Link

Anyplace where the job is displayed in the system, namely the Start page, Notification Jobs page, and Survey Builder page, you may access your responses by clicking the Responses link in the Actions area to the right. You will be taken to the Responses tab, as shown below, with the displayed responses filtered.

The Responses Tab

The Reponses tab can be accessed by clicking the Notifications tab followed by the Responses sub tab.

In the Display Options section at the top of the page, there is a drop down menu that allows you to filter the responses in the Responses section by which job they are associated with. Checking the check box in the Unplayed section, right below the drop down menu, allows you to view only the responses that have not yet been played.

In the Responses section of the page, by default, you will find a list of all of the responses currently in the system. The associated data will be displayed for each response as well as the Actions you may take regarding each.

  • ID:  If the recipient has an ID in the system's database, such as a Student ID or Employee ID, it will be displayed here.

  • First Name: The recipient's first name.

  • Last Name: The recipient's last name.

  • Phone: The phone number where the recipient received the notification or survey message.

  • Message Name: The name of the message or survey to which the recipient responded.

  • Job Name: The name of the job that contained the message to which the recipient responded.

  • Date: The date of the response.

  • Status: Displays whether or not the response has been played.

  • Actions: Click the play link to hear the response. Click Delete to delete the response from the system. Note: Deleting cannot be undone.
